# Wave 1 — Rest-Ambush 5× Reduction

**Status: SIGNED OFF (user, 2026-07-03)** — sweep as computed below (rounding accepted).
Pack size (`max`/`difficulty`) stays vanilla in this component; it is decided per-chapter/
per-map like the zero-ambush areas. Supersedes the prior-agent proposal `design/01`.
**Scope:** the global sweep only. Per-area zero-ambush decisions come later, chapter by chapter.

## Decided (user, 2026-07-03)
- Reduce the **felt** per-rest interrupt chance ~5× across all SoD areas with an active
  random rest table.
- Zero-ambush areas: per-chapter decision, not part of this sweep.

## Mechanism recap (verified, `research/01`)
The engine rolls the listed day/night % **per in-game hour** → felt-per-8h-rest
`= 1−(1−c/100)⁸`. Reduction is computed on the felt rate and inverted back:
`new_c = round(100·(1−(1−felt_old/5)^(1/8)))`, floor 1.

## Open questions (need your call before implementation)
1. **Pack size.** `max 3–6 @ difficulty 80/200` means every ambush still spawns at max —
   doubled on Insane (up to 12 in the crypt). This sweep only changes *how often*, not
   *how many*. Reduce `max`/`difficulty` globally too, or leave size per-chapter alongside
   the zero-lists?
2. **Rounding.** Strict 5× puts the worst areas (BD5110, BD0064…) at 2/2 (felt 15%) and the
   rest at 1 (felt 8%). Accept as computed, or flatten everything to 1?

## Implementation sketch (after sign-off)
One WeiDU component: `COPY_EXISTING` each listed `BD*.are`, follow the rest-struct pointer at
`0xC0`, `WRITE_SHORT +0xA8/+0xAA` (day/night). **Compat-first:** read the *current* value and
apply the reduction formula to it (composes with anything another mod set), guarded by
`enabled && table_count>0`. Pure data patch — no script, no dialogue; safe with SCS/SR/
CDTweaks/EET and vanilla. Applies to areas not yet visited in a running save.
