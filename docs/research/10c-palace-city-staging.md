## 5. Korlasz fight staging candidates

1. **BD0116 palace basement — strongest: vanilla already stages a Korlasz palace rematch.** If she surrendered (`BD_KORLASZ_SURRENDER=1`, BDKORLAS.d states 10/11), she is jailed here; the proximity region **TrapBDKorlasz spans the whole cellar corridor** (bb 378,371–1147,907 — you cross it fetching your impounded gold from Ophyllis) → `BD_KORLAS_FREED=1` → BDKORLAS.baf: she self-buffs (Stoneskin/Minor Globe/Mirror Image), kills guard 1B, downs 1A, opens Door03 → `bdcutkor` → forced dialog states 24-27 ("Now let us finish this!") → `Enemy()`, `BD_SPAWN_KORLASZ=2`, journal **261626 "I must defeat Korlasz once again"**; her death → journal 261627 QUEST_DONE + guard reaction dialogs (BDFIST1A/C). Script complexity LOW (BD0116.baf = 209 lines, mostly barrel/gold bookkeeping). Caveats: fires only on the surrender branch (if killed in BD0130 nothing spawns here), and the area also hosts the doppelganger-cell and treasury quests (coexist fine in vanilla).
2. **BD0118 — a free, pristine stage in the current remix:** duplicate of the BD0100 palace-hall map (4 entrances, 3 doors, empty 0-line script, no regions, excluded from one BDBALDUR timer). Vanilla's one scripted tenant was the Imoen scrying vision; component 225 makes it unreachable. A custom night-fight can therefore be built with zero live contention; enter/exit by script (LeaveAreaLUA), exactly like BDCUT00Z/bdcut09 do.
3. **BD0100 upper hall** — the vanilla hallway-assassin arena; tiny script (133 lines), quiet outside plot 51-55; doors close on cue; even has dormant Imoen bed/floor info regions (vestiges of the assassination's original staging there — cf. `BD_ORIGINAL_ASSASSIN_INTRO`, `bd_001_plot "bd0100"` vars in BDFKSER1.d/BDFENSTE.baf). Good if the new opening merges "assassins + Korlasz" into one night attack.
4. **BD0102 great hall** — grandest room (war council, piano/harp, front doors), but five coordinate-keyed OnCreation cutscene variants (BD0102.baf:21-269) and council NPCs present through plot 52-56: MEDIUM contention, collateral-NPC risk.
5. **BD0104 Flaming Fist cells** — thematically a jail, prologue-quiet script-wise (its 338 lines are almost all chapter-13), but **contradicts canon**: Korlasz's own line 245646 — "I was surprised when I was taken to the palace and not to the jail". Using it requires rewriting her dialog premise; also risks entangling the chapter-13 jail machinery.
6. **Tomb entrance** — not viable without new wiring: BD0120/BD0130 form a closed travel loop with no city-side door; re-entry would need a scripted teleport, and BD0130's script is the heaviest in the prologue (wave spawns).
